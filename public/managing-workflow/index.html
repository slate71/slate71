Managing Workflow

11 Jul, 2023

Managing Engineering Team Workflow Using Kanban `and The Principles of Product Development Flow

Managing an engineering team’s workflow can be a challenging task, especially when it comes to ensuring that the team is productive, efficient, and delivering high-quality work. One approach that has gained popularity in recent years is the use of Kanban, a visual project management tool that helps teams visualize their work, identify bottlenecks, and optimize their workflow. In this blog post, we will explore how to manage an engineering team’s workflow using Kanban and the principles outlined in the book The Principles of Product Development Flow.

The Principles of Product Development Flow

The Principles of Product Development Flow is a book written by Don Reinertsen that provides a comprehensive guide to managing product development. One of the key principles outlined in the book is the importance of managing queues. According to Reinertsen, queues are a fundamental source of waste in product development, and reducing queues can significantly improve workflow efficiency.

To manage queues effectively, Reinertsen recommends using a pull-based system, where work is pulled through the system only when capacity is available, rather than a push-based system, where work is pushed into the system regardless of capacity. This approach helps teams avoid overloading their systems, reduces waiting times, and improves overall efficiency.

Using Kanban to Manage Workflow

Kanban is a visual project management tool that is based on the principles of pull-based systems. Kanban boards typically consist of columns that represent different stages of the workflow, with each column containing cards that represent work items. As work progresses through the workflow, cards are moved from one column to the next, providing a visual representation of the team’s progress.

Kanban boards can be customized to fit the specific needs of the team, with columns representing different stages of the workflow, such as “To Do,” “In Progress,” and “Done.” By limiting the number of cards in each column, the team can avoid overloading their system and ensure that work is pulled through the system only when capacity is available.

Using Flow to Optimize Workflow

Another key principle outlined in The Principles of Product Development Flow is the importance of optimizing workflow using flow. According to Reinertsen, flow is the rate at which work moves through the system, and optimizing flow can significantly improve efficiency.

To optimize flow, Reinertsen recommends using a data-driven approach, where teams use metrics to identify bottlenecks and areas for improvement. By measuring cycle time, lead time, and other key metrics, teams can identify areas where work is getting stuck and take steps to address these issues.

Conclusion

Managing an engineering team’s workflow can be a daunting task, but with the right approach, it can be a rewarding one. By using Kanban to visualize the team’s work and the principles outlined in The Principles of Product Development Flow to manage queues and optimize flow, teams can improve their efficiency, reduce waste, and deliver high-quality work. If you’re looking to manage your team’s workflow more effectively, consider adopting Kanban and the principles of product development flow.
